About

The person behind Frontline DFIR.

Digital forensics and incident response is more than a career path for me. It's the part of security I genuinely can't put down. I love going deep on the Windows artifacts most people overlook, from prefetch and shimcache to event logs, and turning raw evidence into a clear story of what happened on a system.

That curiosity drives my research into Windows internals and endpoint security. I hold CompTIA Security+ and Microsoft AZ-900, completed 13Cubed's Investigating Windows Endpoints, and I'm currently working through Blue Cape Security's Practical Windows Forensic Analyst certification. Frontline DFIR is where I publish what I learn along the way: artifact deep-dives, identity attack analysis, and triage methodology, documented so you can repeat it.

Once PWFA is done, DFIR stays the foundation, but it's not the destination. I'm working toward Windows security research and detection engineering: taking what an investigation teaches you about how an attack actually happened and using it to build the detections that catch the next one earlier.
